Delete The Devs

About

Delete The Devs is a game about deleting the deveveloper crew of the game and as you delete the them, you also delete the game which is the end goal.

The game was built with Unreal together with a team of 9 and 2 external music students assigned by FutureGames.

Info

Role: Gameplay & Animation Programmer

Team Size: 11

Time: 4 Weeks

Engine: Unreal

Introduction

Our first project without traditional leads, artists, QA's, and UX presented a unique challenge. However, we were fortunate to collaborate with two external music students, which added a fresh perspective. We also experimented with two game prototypes, which were later voted on and selected. In the absence of an art team, I took on the responsibilities of animation and gameplay development. Our team utilized Perforce for source control, Miro for prototyping, and Jira for project management.

Gameplay

Our initial prototype utilized artificial intelligence, which I worked on during our AI course. Although the project was ultimately scrapped, I gained valuable experience with key concepts like behaviour trees, search spaces, and graph theory, all of which I applied to the project.

When a crew member is deleted, the crew list widget is dynamically updated to remove their associated work. Previously, a separate function was created for each crew member by one of our designers, but I refactored the code to use a single, reusable function. Additionally, I modified the third-person blueprint to a first-person controller, allowing us to leverage Unreal Engine's built-in gun functionality.

Animation

Our team opted to utilize online animation packs, as none of us had experience with animation. Personally, I relied on mixamo , a trusted animation platform, to bring our project to life.

What I learned

I learned that seeking feedback from others is crucial, as what may seem complete to me may not be perceived the same way by others. Additionally, I acquired a significant amount of knowledge about Unreal Engine.
