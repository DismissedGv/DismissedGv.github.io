Wrathful Hedgehog

A first-person horde shooter about defending your home from foxes. I worked on enemy AI, wave flow, health, UI, and cave pressure.

My work

Introduction

I was responsible for the game's gameplay and enemy AI. The goal was to make a simple horde shooter loop work clearly: enemies spawn, chase the player, attack, and give the player enough time to prepare for the next wave.

I worked with Perforce, Miro, and Jira in a 14-person Futuregames team.

Gameplay

I worked on player health, basic UI feedback, and the systems around each round. When enemies reached the player in their attack state, they could deal damage through collision checks.

The game manager controlled the round flow. One phase spawned enemies and applied pressure through the shrinking cave, while the next gave the player time to recover before another wave started.

Artificial Intelligence

This was my first time working with AI in Unity. I used Unity AI Navigation, NavMesh, and NavMeshAgent so enemies could path toward the player and move around the cave.

I also implemented the enemy spawner. It communicated with the game manager to know when to spawn enemies and how many should appear during a wave.

What I learned

This project taught me how important it is to test AI constantly in the editor. Small changes to movement, attack range, or spawn timing can quickly change the feel of the whole game.

It also taught me to ask questions early instead of assuming everyone understands a feature the same way. Code reviews helped me see different ways to structure gameplay logic.
