My work

Introduction

Biochem Nightshift was a horror puzzle game built in Unreal with a 16-person Futuregames team. I worked as the team's product owner.

I helped guide priorities, communication, and production while also contributing gameplay work where the project needed it.

Product ownership

My role was to keep the team aligned and turn unclear production problems into decisions the team could act on.

I also supported the team with Perforce, planning, and source-control structure so development stayed organized.

Puzzle work

I implemented the marble maze puzzle using collision, goal detection, key reward logic, and a possession handoff back to the player.

The puzzle needed to be understandable in the middle of a horror game, so the implementation had to support both interaction and pacing.

What I learned

This project taught me how important communication and prioritization are when a team is under pressure. I also learned that saying no can protect the game just as much as adding a feature.
