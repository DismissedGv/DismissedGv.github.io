My work

Introduction

Iron League was a mobile Unity project made with Futuregames in collaboration with Turborilla. I worked as the gameplay lead on a 17-person team.

My main responsibility was making player feedback clear through audio, score updates, and UI responses during gameplay.

Audio

I built the audio manager and volume handling so player settings could persist between sessions and stay easy to control from the menus.

I also made the setup easier for designers to use, so sounds could be assigned and adjusted without digging through gameplay code.

Score feedback

I worked on score initialization, live score updates, and score popup feedback. The goal was to make scoring feel immediate and readable on mobile.

The UI needed to stay simple, but still give the player enough feedback to understand what changed and when.

What I learned

This project taught me a lot about working in a larger team, keeping systems designer-friendly, and communicating clearly when several disciplines depend on the same features.
